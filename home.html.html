The pharmacy dispenses medications, manages drug storage, monitors expiry dates, and provides dosage instructions. Pharmacists also maintain stock records to prevent shortages.

Pharmacy

Amoxicillin 500mg

Treatment: Bacterial infections such as throat infections, ear infections, and urinary tract infections.

Usage: Take orally with water, usually every 8 hours.

Dosage: Adults: 500mg every 8 hours; Children: dose based on weight.

Other Notes: Side effects may include mild nausea or diarrhea. Take after meals to reduce stomach upset. Prescription required. Avoid if allergic to penicillin.

Paracetamol

Treatment: Relief of mild to moderate pain and fever.

Usage: Take orally with water. Can be taken with or without food.

Dosage: Adults: 500mg-1000mg every 4-6 hours (max 4g/day). Children: based on weight.

Other Notes: Side effects are rare but may include liver damage if overdosed. OTC medicine. Avoid alcohol while taking.

Tylenol

Treatment: Pain relief for headaches, muscle aches, and fever.

Usage: Take orally with water. Can be taken with or without food.

Dosage: Adults: 500mg-1000mg every 4-6 hours (max 4g/day). Children: weight-based dosing.

Other Notes: Side effects are rare but may include liver issues if overdosed. OTC medicine. Avoid alcohol. Keep out of reach of children.
